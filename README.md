# Students-Social-Media-Addiction-Analysis

![Capture](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c4700acb-e359-4807-a127-81abac0fc399)

### Overview
This project presents a descriptive and exploratory analysis of student's social media usage patterns and their potential impact on academic performance.The analysis includes data from 705 students across multiple countries, exploring variables such as addiction scores, daily usage, platform preferences, gender, age, and relationship status.The goal was to identify patterns and insights that could help educators and policymakers understand social media behaviors among students and their effects on academics.
### Tools Used
- Excel: Data cleaning, preparation, and aggregation to ensure consistency and accuracy.
- Power BI: Built an interactive dashboard to visualize trends, patterns, and correlations in student's social media usage.
### Key Insights
- Addiction Scores by Country: India and USA show the highest levels of social media addiction.
- Platform Usage: Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook are the most widely used platforms among students.
- Academic Performance: A significant number of students reported that social media affects their academic performance.
- Daily Usage Patterns: Female students tend to spend slightly more average hours on social media than male students.
- Relationship Status: Single students show higher average daily usage compared to those in relationships or with complicated statuses.
- Age and Addiction Score: Younger students tend to have higher addiction scores.
### Recommendations
- Awareness Programs: Schools and universities can organize workshops to raise awareness of the effects of social media addiction.
- Balanced Usage: Encourage students to manage daily social media usage to minimize academic impact.
- Platform-Specific Strategies: Focus on highly addictive platforms like Instagram and TikTok when designing interventions.
- Further Research: Conduct longitudinal studies to track changes in addiction and academic performance over time.
